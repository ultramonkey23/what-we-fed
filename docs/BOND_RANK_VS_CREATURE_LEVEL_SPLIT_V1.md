- This file defines a hard lane split between Bond Rank and Creature Level.
- This file does not replace timing/lane combat doctrine or world consequence canon.

## Confirmed Shared Foundation

- First bond permanently unlocks species.
- Bond continues after unlock as Bond Rank.
- Bond milestones are rare, major, and identity-defining.
- Creature Level is a separate practical growth lane.
- Meaningful evolution belongs primarily to Bond milestones, not routine levels.
- Creature systems must preserve dark invasive archive identity, not mascot collection loops.

## Why This Split Matters

- Without a hard split, bond drifts into generic XP leveling.
- Without a hard split, level design steals creature identity evolution.
- Players lose emotional clarity if both lanes grant the same kinds of rewards.
- System overlap creates tuning sludge: two tracks paying for the same outcome.
- Canon drift weakens replay value by turning creatures into stat containers.

## Bond Rank Definition

- Bond Rank exists only after first species unlock.
- First unlock is not Bond Rank; it is a one-time acquisition event.
- Bond Rank is milestone-based, low-frequency, premium.
- Bond Rank is emotionally heavy: sequence trust, archive attunement, invasive intimacy.
- Bond Rank is the primary owner of meaningful evolution.
- Bond Rank should pay for:
  - meaningful form shifts
  - major support behavior rewrites
  - identity breakthroughs
  - role divergence and branch commitments
  - signature evolutions
  - major instinct/archetype expression
  - major archive/covenant milestones
- Bond Rank must not be framed as bigger routine numbers.

## Creature Level Definition

- Creature Level is a separate post-unlock lane.
- Creature Level is frequent, readable, and system-oriented.
- Creature Level handles practical development between Bond milestones.
- Creature Level should pay for:
  - support efficiency
  - stat growth
  - cooldown adjustments
  - effect strength tuning
  - role tuning and practical specialization
  - smaller mastery gains
- Creature Level must not own major species identity transformation.

## What Belongs to Bond Rank

- Any change that redefines "what this creature fundamentally is now."
- Major evolution states and signature forms.
- New instinct doctrine, archetype expression, or covenant depth.
- Branch-lock decisions with real identity consequence.
- Large behavior-package rewrites for support actions.
- Archive milestones that mark relationship phase changes.

## What Belongs to Creature Level

- Any change that improves "how this creature performs right now."
- Incremental stat profile growth.
- Cooldown/uptime and effect-strength tuning.
- Role efficiency and handling reliability.
- Practical specialization within an existing identity.
- Repeatable progression beats that keep runs rewarding.

## What Must Never Be Shared Sloppily

- Do not grant major form identity shifts from normal levels.
- Do not use Bond milestones for routine +X% stat plumbing.
- Do not gate baseline combat viability behind Bond milestones.
- Do not let both lanes unlock the same reward class with different names.
- Do not let Bond become frequent and noisy.
- Do not let Level become filler while all excitement sits in Bond.
- Do not use mirrored UX language that implies both lanes are equivalent.

## Emotional Read of Each Lane

- Bond Rank read: "We crossed a line. This species relationship changed forever."
- Creature Level read: "I refined execution. This creature performs better now."
- Bond should feel rare, ceremonial, invasive, consequential.
- Level should feel steady, legible, tactical, dependable.

## Suggested Pacing Philosophy

- Bond milestone count target per species line (post-unlock): 3 to 5 total.
- Preferred baseline: 4 Bond milestones for most species lines.
- Milestone spacing: widen across the line (early achievable, later demanding).
- Normal levels should occur many times between Bond milestones (roughly 4 to 8 levels per Bond step equivalent of progress cadence).
- Keep Bond premium without stinginess:
  - always show clear Bond progress vectors
  - avoid hidden friction spikes
  - guarantee at least one predictable route to next milestone
- Keep Level satisfying without boredom:
  - each level should change visible performance
  - alternate reward emphasis (offense, cadence, stability, utility)
  - keep level rewards meaningful but non-transformational

## Suggested Presentation Philosophy

- Bond presentation:
  - ceremonial transitions, authored text, archive-language verbs (attune, rupture, converge, consecrate)
  - fewer popups, stronger moments
  - explicit milestone naming (Bond Rank I/II/III/IV, or named rites)
- Level presentation:
  - compact HUD-safe readouts, numbers, short practical descriptors
  - immediate before/after clarity (+cooldown recovery, +support uptime, +effect output)
  - frequent but quiet reinforcement
- Keep language distinct:
  - Bond words: rite, sequence, attunement, covenant, metamorphosis
  - Level words: calibration, throughput, handling, efficiency, tuning
- Avoid confusion:
  - separate UI panels and icon families
  - separate audio/visual cues
  - never summarize both lanes under one generic "growth" header

## What Should Be Promoted

- First unlock as a one-time premium species event.
- Bond Rank as the major evolution owner.
- Creature Level as practical, regular, and useful.
- Clear reward ownership per lane before implementation.
- Distinct UX language and visuals per lane.

## What Should Be Softened

- Bond progress that behaves like constant XP drip.
- Level rewards that are pure dead-number inflation.
- Any copy that treats Bond and Level as interchangeable synonyms.
- Over-frequent Bond moments that erode milestone weight.

## What Should Be Retired

- Designs where normal levels trigger major identity metamorphosis.
- Designs where Bond milestones are mostly stat bumps.
- Combined growth UI that obscures lane ownership.
- "Progression blob" implementation where inputs/outputs are not lane-tagged.

## Validation

- A feature is invalid if it does not declare lane owner: Bond Rank or Creature Level.
- An evolution proposal is invalid if its primary trigger is Creature Level.
- A practical tuning proposal is invalid if it requires a Bond milestone.
- A UI proposal is invalid if lane identity cannot be understood in under 3 seconds.
- A reward model is invalid if both lanes pay for the same class of outcome.
- A pacing model is invalid if Bond steps are frequent enough to feel routine.
- A pacing model is invalid if Level steps are too weak to change play feel.

- Normal levels must grant practical gains that do not require Bond milestones.
- Bond emotional read: transformational relationship threshold crossed.
- Level emotional read: operational proficiency improved.
- UI language, iconography, and feedback channels must stay distinct per lane.
- No duplicated reward class across both lanes.

PACING_CONTRACT:
- Default Bond milestone count per species line: 4 (acceptable range: 3 to 5).
- Bond spacing widens over time; later steps require materially deeper commitment.
- Levels occur substantially more often than Bond milestones.
- Bond remains premium via clarity and consequence, not via obscurity or starvation.
- Level remains satisfying via visible practical impact, not filler increments.

- Do not merge Bond and Level into one growth currency.
- Do not let Bond milestones become bigger-number wrappers.
- Do not let Level become the primary source of creature meaning.

- Preserve long-run replay value by keeping both lanes useful and non-redundant.
