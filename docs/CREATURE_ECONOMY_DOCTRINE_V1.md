## Confirmed Core Rules

- The player is the Living Codex: an invasive archive and wound, not a collector mascot.
- Bond vs Eat remains the defining creature relationship split.
- DNA economy and species meaning remain central; creature systems are ecosystem identity, not reward sludge.
- Progression lanes must stay discrete and named; no fuzzy merged lane logic.
- Evolution must be meaningful and milestone-driven, not passive number inflation.

## System Goals

- Make creature progression readable at first glance: each lane has one clear job.
- Make growth scalable for hundreds of hours without infinite-stat collapse.
- Preserve dark authored tone: predatory archive, invasive signal, consequence-rich choices.
- Keep repeat encounters valuable forever without fake rebonding loops.
- Keep implementation clean: each reward input maps to a specific lane sink.

## Current Friction

- First unlock, bond progression, level growth, and long-tail chase are currently easy to blur.
- Repeat species can feel low-value after unlock if sinks are not explicit.
- Bond progression risks drifting into generic XP grind if milestones are not protected.
- Pre-unlock predation tension can become opaque punishment if not surfaced clearly.
- Post-max progression risks infinite bond levels instead of a curated rare-specimen lane.

## Lane Breakdown

- Lane 1 — Initial Bond Unlock: premium one-time species acquisition; never a normal level track.
- Lane 2 — Predation Debt / Bond Resistance: pre-unlock tension lane; raises bond friction without hard-locking.
- Lane 3 — Bond Rank: post-unlock milestone lane; primary home of meaningful evolution.
- Lane 4 — Creature Level: practical continuous performance lane; never identity evolution.
- Lane 5 — Creature Potential: metaprogression lane; long-horizon species-line depth and ceilings.
- Lane 6 — Repeat Species Value: post-unlock routing lane; keeps repeated encounters permanently useful.
- Lane 7 — Post-Max Rare Specimen Hunt: long-tail exceptional specimen lane after max bond; replaces infinite bond levels.

## Initial Bond Unlock

- Purpose: establish first-time species bond as a premium archive event.
- It does:
  - Permanently unlock species entry in the archive after first successful bond.
  - Preserve species availability forever once unlocked.
  - Frame the moment as "new sequence acquired," not "level up."
- It must never do:
  - Never repeat as a farmable rebond loop.
  - Never function as a stat bump lane.
  - Never be revoked by later outcomes.
- Progression type: one-time milestone acquisition.
- In-world framing: Archive ignition, first successful sequence stabilization.

## Predation Debt / Bond Resistance

- Purpose: preserve pre-unlock moral tension between eating now and bonding later.
- It does:
  - Track pre-unlock species predation as Predation Debt.
  - Increase first-bond resistance with readable, bounded pressure.
  - Create species history with the player before archive entry.
- It must never do:
  - Never create hidden punishment math.
  - Never hard-lock first bond under normal play.
  - Never persist as debt after successful first unlock.
- Progression type: temporary resistance curve before Lane 1 completion.
- In-world framing: species marrow remembers intrusion; signal handshake becomes harder, not forbidden.

## Bond Rank

- Purpose: define post-unlock premium relational ascent and species-line breakthroughs.
- It does:
  - Continue progression after first unlock in rare milestone steps.
  - Trigger major identity events at each milestone.
  - Drive form shifts, instinct rewrites, support behavior upgrades, and signature evolutions.
- It must never do:
  - Never become infinite normal XP leveling.
  - Never degrade into tiny repeated number bumps.
  - Never be replaced by Creature Level.
- Progression type: low-frequency, high-impact milestone track (premium cadence).
- In-world framing: deeper signal attunement between Codex and preserved sequence.

## Creature Level

- Purpose: provide regular practical growth and tuning without stealing identity evolution.
- It does:
  - Raise practical performance values and support efficiency.
  - Support specialization and regular combat-readiness tuning.
  - Offer ongoing readable progression cadence between bond milestones.
- It must never do:
  - Never unlock species.
  - Never trigger major evolution forms.
  - Never define species identity breakthroughs.
- Progression type: frequent system-oriented incremental growth.
- In-world framing: handling discipline, execution refinement, support throughput tuning.

## Creature Potential

- Purpose: represent unrealized becoming across long-horizon metaprogression.
- It does:
  - Deepen species lineage value over many runs.
  - Influence ceilings for evolution depth, lineage expression, and rare specimen range.
  - Increase long-term archive value for invested species lines.
- It must never do:
  - Never act as direct raw power replacement for level or bond.
  - Never collapse into generic "bigger stats" lane.
  - Never obscure which lane actually caused a growth event.
- Progression type: slow meta accrual with ceiling and complexity effects.
- In-world framing: unresolved sequence depth; the archive senses unused form futures.

## Repeat Species Value

- Purpose: guarantee repeated species encounters remain meaningful after unlock.
- Valid post-unlock routes:
  - Eat: immediate run power and predatory acceleration.
  - Creature Level input: practical growth support.
  - Bond milestone input: contributes to next Bond Rank threshold.
  - Potential input: long-horizon lineage deepening.
  - Species mastery: behavior knowledge and handling reliability.
  - Archive depth: richer codex value and species insight layers.
  - Rare chase support: enables post-max exceptional specimen conditions.
- It must never do:
  - Never use fake rebonding of an already unlocked species.
  - Never allow repeated encounters to collapse into junk value.
  - Never blur lane ownership of rewards.
- Progression type: routing layer that feeds other valid sinks.
- In-world framing: each encounter adds ink to an existing sequence ledger.

## Post-Max Rare Specimen Hunt

- Purpose: provide long-tail excitement after max Bond Rank without infinite bond inflation.
- It does:
  - Unlock only after max Bond Rank for that species line.
  - Surface uncommon exceptional specimens with meaningful expression differences.
  - Deepen species lines through quality, variant, and lineage-grade extremes.
- It must never do:
  - Never continue infinite normal bond levels.
  - Never become default collector mimicry language or logic.
  - Never replace core Bond Rank identity milestones.
- Progression type: uncommon high-value chase lane for exceptional specimens.
- Canon language:
  - Specimen Quality
  - Variant Form
  - Lineage Grade
  - Signal Purity
- Preservation rule: if a preservation tool exists, it must read as archive apparatus (for example, Sequence Stasis), not a capture-ball analog.

## What Evolution Belongs To

- Primary home: Bond Rank milestones.
- Evolution triggers:
  - Bond milestone completion unlocks meaningful evolution steps.
  - Potential may gate ceiling eligibility and branch depth.
- Non-triggers:
  - Creature Level never triggers major evolution.
  - Potential never independently triggers evolution events.
  - Initial Bond Unlock never substitutes for post-unlock evolution milestones.
- Outcome rule: major form and role breakthroughs are paid by Bond Rank, not by grind lanes.

## What Should Be Promoted

- First unlock as a premium species acquisition event.
- Predation Debt as readable tension that creates species memory.
- Bond Rank milestones as rare identity-defining breakthroughs.
- Creature Level as clean practical tuning lane.
- Creature Potential as long-horizon species-line meta depth.
- Post-max rare specimen hunt as curated long-tail chase.
- Repeat-species routing clarity so no encounter feels wasted.

## What Should Be Softened

- Raw bond-XP drip behavior between milestones.
- Milestone rewards that are only bigger numbers.
- Overly punitive pre-unlock predation penalties.
- Any presentation that makes Level appear equivalent to Bond Rank.
- Any system wording that makes Potential sound like flat power.

## What Should Be Retired

- Fake rebonding loops for already unlocked species.
- Infinite normal bond levels after max bond.
- Opaque pre-unlock punishment without readable cause/effect.
- Borrowed primary language such as "IV" and "shiny."
- Generic collector-game framing that weakens invasive archive tone.

## What Should Be Deferred

- Exact UI implementation of Predation Debt surfaces.
- Final preservation apparatus design for rare specimens.
- Final taxonomy visuals for Variant Form and Lineage Grade expression.
- High-end Potential visualization and archive presentation layers.
- Cross-system integration depth with later world-state escalation canon.

## Validation

- Every creature progression feature must declare exactly one primary lane owner.
- Every reward must declare its destination sink before implementation.
- If a reward feeds multiple lanes, one lane must be primary and others secondary routed inputs.
- Evolution proposals are invalid unless they bind to Bond Rank milestones.
- Creature Level proposals are invalid if they introduce major evolution identity shifts.
- Potential proposals are invalid if they produce direct immediate power spikes.
- Pre-unlock predation logic is invalid if it hard-locks first bond in normal play.
- Repeat species content is invalid if no lasting value route is provided.
- Post-max content is invalid if it extends infinite normal bond tiers.
- Terminology is invalid if it drifts into collector-game mimic terms as primary canon.

- Do not merge bond, level, potential, and specimen lanes into one fuzzy progression blob.
- Do not let leveling steal the job of Bond Rank.
- Do not trivialize bond milestones into number bumps.
- Do not make repeat species worthless after first unlock.
- Do not use infinite post-max bond tiers.
- Do not drift into obvious collector-game mimic language or structure.
- Keep creature meaning tied to ecosystem identity and archive consequence.
- All Bond-vs-Level decisions must satisfy BOND_RANK_VS_CREATURE_LEVEL_SPLIT_V1 without exception.

LONG_PLAY_RULE:
- For 100+ hour stability, each repeated species encounter must always have at least one meaningful route now (run power or level), one medium route (bond progress or mastery), and one long route (potential or rare hunt support).
