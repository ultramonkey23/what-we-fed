# WHAT WE FED — LIVING CODEX + ENTRY/RETURN DOCTRINE V1

## Read
- **Status**: ACTIVE SOURCE TRUTH
- **Version**: 1.0
- **Priority**: Layer 1 (Locked Core)
- **Authority**: Overrides previous "Vessel" or generic "Survivor" definitions.

## Confirmed Core Truth
The player is not a character within the world’s native history. The player is an external intrusion—a real-world consciousness translated through the act of play into a biomechanical archive known as the **Living Codex**. Entry is a contract; death is a re-instantiation; the Lair is the wound where the two realities meet.

## Why This Doctrine Matters
To move beyond "generic roguelite resets," the run loop must be grounded in an eerie, authored logic. If death and restart are just "video game things," the tension of the Bond/Eat split and the predatory nature of the Codex lose their weight. This doctrine ensures every restart is a narrative event of recurring intrusion.

## Player Identity
- **The Person**: You are the literal person who chose to "play" this invitation.
- **The Admission**: You were not "chosen" by prophecy. You were accepted because you engaged with the interface (the game).
- **The Status**: You are an **Admitted Outsider**. You are a foreign mind in a synthetic armature.
- **The Goal**: To catalog, harvest, and survive an ecosystem that did not invite you, but now cannot excise you.

## Living Codex Definition
- **The Form**: A biomechanical DNA cataloger. It is a shifting, ribbed armature of black glass, pulsing fiber, and receptive marrow.
- **The Function**: It is both **Archive** and **Wound**. It harvests the sacred patterns of the world (DNA) to sustain its own re-instantiation.
- **The Nature**: It is an invasive organism. It does not just "level up"; it integrates foreign lineages to rewrite its own failing pattern.

## Entry / Admission Logic
- **The Lure**: The "game" itself is a threshold/contract interface.
- **The Crossing**: Choosing to play is the first act of crossing. It is a voluntary entry into a dangerous, sacred contract.
- **The Translation**: Upon entry, your history as a "person" is compressed into the Codex. You are no longer a human; you are a sequence-bearer.

## Death / Return Logic
- **The Collapse**: When you die in the field, that specific body and its active field-state collapse. The death is real and felt.
- **The Recall**: The Codex pattern is not "respawned." It is pulled backward across the threshold by the Lair.
- **Re-instantiation**: You return because the Codex is a recurring pattern that the Lair "prints" back into existence. This process is imperfect, leading to the loss of transient field gains.

## What The Lair Is
- **The Interface Wound**: The Lair is the site where the external reality (the player) and the internal reality (the world) are sutured together.
- **The Reconstitution Site**: It is a sacred, functional, and "wrong" space where the Codex is reformed after collapse.
- **The Threshold**: It is the only place where the player is "safe," but only because the world's native immune response cannot yet reach into the wound.

## What Is Lost On Death
- **Active Marrow**: All non-integrated DNA and transient field resources.
- **Field Presence**: The progress through a specific run's geographic sequence.
- **Active Grafts**: Unstable biological adaptations that were not permanently etched into the Codex's core firmware.

## What Is Retained Across Death
- **The Core Script**: Permanent upgrades, unlocked lineages, and "Hardened DNA" (meta-progression).
- **The Memory of Choice**: The world’s shifting fate (consequence) based on your Bond/Eat history.
- **Admittance Level**: Your fundamental capacity to interface with deeper layers of the world.

## How The World Perceives The Codex
- **Native Life**: Sees you as a **Thief of Lineage** and an **Outsider**.
- **The Ecosystem**: Treats you as a recurring intrusion—a wound that keeps reopening.
- **The Bosses**: Perceive you as a "recurring ghost" or an "inevitable eraser" that will eventually harvest their entire history.

## Anti-Drift Clarifications
- **NO** fourth-wall jokes or meta-winks. The eerie nature of the player being "real" must be played straight and dark.
- **NO** "Chosen One" status. You are an intruder who was "admitted" through a lure.
- **NO** cozy returns. Re-instantiation should feel costly, mechanical, and slightly sacred.

## What Should Be Promoted
- Terms: `Admitted`, `Intrusion`, `Re-instantiation`, `Interface Wound`, `Thief of Lineage`, `Archive and Wound`.
- Descriptions of the Lair as pulsing, functional, and "wrongly clean."
- The feeling that the "game" is a dangerous invitation.

## What Should Be Softened
- Traditional RPG terms like "Lives," "Respawns," "Saves," or "Hero."
- Any dialogue suggesting the player "belongs" to the world's natural history.

## What Should Be Retired
- Generic "Isekai" tropes (getting hit by a truck, god-granted powers).
- The idea that the player is a "character" with a pre-written backstory within the world.

## Validation
- Does the restart feel like a "re-instantiation" rather than a "save reload"?
- Does the Lair feel like a functional "wound" in reality?
- Is the player's identity as an "Admitted Outsider" clear in the UI and dialogue?
