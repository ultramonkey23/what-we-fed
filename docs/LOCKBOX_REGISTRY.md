## Canon Notes
### Player / Creature Separation

The player is separate from creatures.

The player is the active combat anchor: movement, dodge, parry, attack timing, positioning, ultimate trigger, and core combat decisions.

Creatures are bonded modifiers that can transform playstyle through auto-attacks, passives, triggered effects, class-like modifiers, ranged pressure, and support identity.

Creature count is meta progression.

### Combat Rhythm Doctrine

Combat should feel gamey, RPG-like, and action-driven — not turn-based, not fully song-locked, and not strictly lane-bound in presentation.

Music and rhythm should strongly influence visual timing, hit feel, animation snap, feedback, enemy attack cadence, and attack satisfaction, but the song should not rigidly control every combat decision.

The core requirement is:

```text
Player attacks and enemy attacks must feel on beat.
```

Parry, dodge, movement, creature support, RPG modifiers, enemy pressure, and build effects can operate with more real-time action freedom as long as combat remains readable and honest.

Lanes are no longer sacred as visible combat structure. They may survive only as hidden timing/threat scaffolding if they help readability. The evolved combat direction is a freer action-RPG hunting field with beat-feel attacks and readable threat direction.

### World Start

The world begins uncorrupted, safer, strange, beautiful, alive, and worth affecting.

The horror is not the starting point. The horror is what the world can become through feeding, bonding, corruption, ritual pressure, and escalation.

### Spire

The player is moving toward a giant distant Spire.

The Spire should visibly get closer with each level and transform with world state.

The Spire starts beautiful, ancient, and mysterious — not obviously evil.

### Style Doctrine

WHAT WE FED uses **LEGENDARY PIXEL FABLE INK** as the core visual identity: detailed top-down pixel-art monster RPG style inspired by PS1-era JRPGs, GBA fantasy RPGs, classic creature battlers, Golden Sun-like jewel-toned shading, Pokemon-like silhouette readability, and Digimon-like evolution energy.

**Bone Ink / Bonecut Ink** is not the default. It is a later/corrupted treatment for Blight, Omen, boss, high-pressure, or late-run states.

Action assets are authored facing down/south by default. Use idle/base plus one branch frame. Do not require two unique authored frames per action or authored defeat frames.

### 6. LOCKBOX CARD
### Name:
Beat-Feel Combat Loosening

### Goal:
Combat should feel more like an action RPG with rhythmic impact instead of a strict rhythm game. Player attacks and enemy attacks must feel on beat, but the song should not hard-lock every combat action.

### Why it fits WHAT WE FED:
This supports a more gamey RPG direction with stats, creature modifiers, auto-attacks, passives, dodging, parrying, and enemy pressure. Music remains important through animation snap, impact timing, visual pulses, combo feel, enemy attack cadence, and attack satisfaction, but player agency stays active and responsive.

### Implementation status:
LOCKED FOR LATER — needs fresh combat audit first.

### Repo truth dependency:
Pass 1/Gemini must verify current beat/song system, timing windows, attack input gating, parry/dodge gating, CombatMeter, visual beat feedback, enemy attack timing, audio/beat sync code, and whether attacks are currently accepted only on beat or merely scored by timing.

### Risk:
Loosening rhythm too much could erase the game’s timing identity. Keeping rhythm too strict could make the game feel stiff, unreliable, or less RPG-like.

### Minimum future implementation:
Separate hard input permission from beat feel:
- player attacks can be pressed responsively
- player attack animation/impact snaps or emphasizes the nearest beat
- enemy attack windups/releases feel beat-aligned
- on-beat player attacks get better feedback or bonus
- off-beat player attacks still work, but feel weaker or less stylish
- parry/dodge remain responsive real-time defensive tools

### Do not do:
Do not turn combat into turn-based combat. Do not remove timing feel entirely. Do not force every action to wait for the song. Do not make off-beat attacks feel broken or ignored.
