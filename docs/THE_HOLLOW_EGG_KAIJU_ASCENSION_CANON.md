# THE HOLLOW EGG — KAIJU ASCENSION CANON

## Status
Locked as future flagship canon.
Not for immediate implementation.

## Purpose
This file defines the first stable canon for the long-term kaiju ascension arc of **What We Fed**.

It exists to preserve the game’s true final-scale consequence loop:
- the player does not merely grow stronger
- the world does not merely host encounters
- creature choices do not merely affect runs
- something larger is being shaped

The Hollow Egg is one of the game’s primary meaning engines.

---

# 1. Core Concept

## Name
**The Hollow Egg**

## Nature
The Hollow Egg is:
- an embryonic catastrophe
- a dormant world-organism
- an ancient sealed bio-weapon
- a larval apocalypse
- a vessel of accumulation and consequence

It is not dead.
It is not inert.
It is listening, absorbing, and becoming.

The world does not simply contain danger.
It is incubating it.

---

# 2. Narrative / Thematic Role

The Hollow Egg gives the game a long-term world spine.

It ties together:
- predation
- mutation
- creature consequence
- bond vs eat philosophy
- world-state evolution
- boss outcomes
- endgame escalation

Without it, the game is a strong creature-action roguelite.
With it, the game becomes a world-scale consequence machine.

## Core truth (Patch V1)
The player is not only surviving the world; they are intruding upon a living reality.
The **Kaiju** is not your literal evil side or a simple boss.
- **Immune Response**: The Kaiju is the world's reaction to you as an outsider (the Codex).
- **Intrusion at Scale**: Every run increases the world's awareness of you.
- **The World is Alive**: The planet responds to your "Entry" with corrective pressure.

---

# 3. What Shapes the Egg

The Hollow Egg should not evolve randomly.
Its development is shaped by the player’s behavior and the state of the world.

## Major influence channels

### Predation
- heavy creature consumption
- ecosystem collapse
- ruthless short-term self-growth
- boss killing without restraint
- feeding the world’s hunger

### Bond
- preserving dangerous life
- creature training and protection
- building support networks
- investment in relational power
- choosing continuity over immediate gain

### Destabilization
- regional corruption
- failed containment
- leaving major threats unresolved
- biome collapse
- feeding chaos indirectly

### Apex Pressure
- major combat feats
- elite and boss outcomes
- high-tier run mutations
- world-defining events
- repeated escalation toward dominance

These channels should not all mean the same thing.
They should push the Egg toward different futures.

---

# 4. Omen States

Before the kaiju ever fully manifests, the world should show signs.

## State 0 — Buried Silence
The Egg is dormant.
The world gives only faint warning.

Possible signs:
- strange pulses in quiet spaces
- malformed creature births
- shell-like motifs where they should not exist
- Quig making jokes that feel too specific
- codex fragments that hint at something gestating below everything

## State 1 — Hairline Hunger
The first pressure leaks outward.

Possible signs:
- altered enemy aggression
- wrong-sounding creature cries
- eggshell textures appearing in boss spaces
- faint tremors or resonance
- predator species behaving as if drawn somewhere unseen

## State 2 — Internal Stirring
The world begins reorganizing around the coming birth.

Possible signs:
- sky changes
- pressure shifts in specific regions
- escalating ecological imbalance
- creature behavior divergence
- unstable weather or environmental distortion
- rumors, rituals, or carcass-fields

## State 3 — Near-Hatch Catastrophe
The world understands something is coming, even if it cannot name it.

Possible signs:
- giant shell fragments
- impossible silhouettes
- dead zones / overgrown zones
- boss instability
- altered regional music/vibe
- specific species becoming easier or harder to bond/eat
- the world behaving like a womb under stress

These omen states should eventually be reflected in:
- environments
- enemies
- bosses
- UI flavor
- Quig commentary
- codex entries
- run/world structure

---

# 5. Kaiju Growth Stages

The Hollow Egg should eventually progress through a real transformation line.

## Stage 1 — Dormant Egg
Hidden, sealed, or buried.
Almost no direct interaction.

## Stage 2 — Disturbance
The shell or surrounding system begins to react.
The world starts to bend.

## Stage 3 — Juvenile Threat
The thing has enough presence to affect regions, species, and boss behavior.
It is no longer theoretical.

## Stage 4 — Apex Destabilizer
It becomes a world-shaping force.
Its existence changes ecology and encounter logic.

## Stage 5 — Awakening Catastrophe
The world reaches a threshold where the Egg is no longer containable.
The game begins pivoting toward end-state pressure.

## Stage 6 — Kaiju-Class Final Catastrophe
The final form is not a fixed boss template.
It is the culmination of:
- player philosophy
- creature consequence
- ecological damage or preservation
- boss outcomes
- world-state direction

The final kaiju should feel earned, not arbitrary.

---

# 6. Final-Boss Design Rule

The kaiju is not just “the final boss.”
It is:

- the world’s answer to what was fed into it
- a mirror of player philosophy
- an apocalypse with memory
- the largest embodiment of the game’s themes

## Core design law
The final kaiju should feel like:

**the world learned from the player.**

This means its:
- role
- demeanor
- pressure style
- audiovisual identity
- world effect
- thematic meaning

should all reflect the path taken to reach it.

---

# 7. Relationship To The Rest Of The Game

## Bond vs Eat
The Hollow Egg should deepen the meaning of both sides.

- Eating feeds immediate predation and may accelerate certain catastrophic tendencies.
- Bonding preserves dangerous life and may shift the Egg toward other outcomes.
- Neither path should be simplified into “good” or “evil.”
- The game should preserve moral tension and strategic ambiguity.

## Creatures
Creatures are not isolated systems.
They are part of a larger living world.
Their preservation, extinction, mutation, and use should help shape the Egg’s destiny.

## Ranch
The ranch may later become one of the few places where the player:
- deliberately preserves important creature lines
- prepares support creatures for future world consequences
- resists total collapse
- studies or reacts to omen-state change

## Bosses
Bosses should eventually matter beyond loot and obstacle value.
Their deaths, survival, corruption, or feeding consequences should influence the world and help shape the Hollow Egg’s development.

## World State
The Hollow Egg and evolving world state must eventually work together.
They are not separate features.
They are part of the same consequence layer.

---

# 8. Tone Rules

The Hollow Egg system must feel:
- eerie
- large-scale
- consequential
- ominous
- patient
- terrible
- mythic and iconic
- premium menace over raw gore

It must NOT feel:
- random
- purely mechanical
- tacked on
- melodramatic without payoff
- disconnected from the creature systems
- disconnected from the player’s choices
- like generic superhero apocalypse gloss
- like bright-clean anime spectacle detached from predatory creature logic

## Influence vectors for late-scope tone (not copy targets)
When this system is eventually implemented, tone shaping may borrow vectors from:
- Solo Leveling for shadowed authority and ascendant menace
- Digimon for creature-evolution consequence and form identity
- My Hero Academia for clear readable impact language at large scale
- Ben 10 for distinct transformation-form identity

These are direction vectors only and must remain filtered through WHAT WE FED's dark creature-consequence spine.
