# WHAT WE FED — FINAL GAME SCOPE CANON (FLAGSHIP VERSION)

## Purpose

This file defines the **true flagship scope** of the game.

Its job is to tell the project, future chats, and coding agents:

- what the game ultimately is
- what the player fantasy is
- what systems belong in the game
- what systems should be deferred until later
- what the long-term world and boss scope really are
- what must be preserved as development continues
- what kind of game this project must never drift away from

This is a **canon scope file**, not a loose brainstorm.
It should be treated as the current highest-level project spine until deliberately revised.

# 1. Final Game Identity

## Title
**WHAT WE FED**

## Final genre identity
A dark, stylish, creature-driven **RPG roguelite** where realtime lane-timing combat is the signature expression layer for predation, build identity, and monstrous ascent.

## Style evolution note
The project direction has evolved from a more brutal horror-first emphasis toward a **dark cool creature power fantasy** emphasis.
This is an evolution of focus, not a genre swap.
The game must remain dangerous, strange, predatory, and readable.

## Genre hierarchy
- RPG first
- Roguelite second
- Creature-driven progression game third
- Lane-timing action combat fourth, as the signature expression layer

## Layer responsibilities

### Combat is responsible for
- honest moment-to-moment skill pressure
- readable three-lane threat management
- proving whether the build actually functions under song-time
- making creature growth visible and earned

### The RPG layer is responsible for
- deciding what kind of creature the player is becoming
- turning DNA, tendencies, support, and bond vs eat choices into identity
- producing transformational growth instead of flat stat inflation

### The roguelite layer is responsible for
- structuring repeatable runs under escalating pressure
- creating adaptation, variation, and failure-driven learning
- supporting replayability without generic content sprawl

### The creature layer is responsible for
- giving progression a body, appetite, and consequence
- preserving species-specific DNA meaning
- keeping bond vs eat central to the player fantasy

## Short pitch
A weak, hungry being survives a hostile world through timing mastery, predation, and dangerous creature bonds, shaping a feared monster across repeatable song-runs where every bond, meal, and mutation changes what the creature becomes.

## Core fantasy
**Start weak. Become feared.**

## Core loop hierarchy

### In-run loop
- read lanes
- survive timing pressure
- prey on species for DNA
- decide bond or eat when creature offers appear
- steer the creature toward a recognizable role before the song ends

### Between-level loop (within a run)
- **Run shape:** **9 regular levels** + **1 boss level**.
- **Regular levels:** realtime lane combat for an **authored segment under ~2 minutes** of the track (slice, not full song), then **menus**: reward selection → inventory / resource management → next level.
- **Boss level:** **full song** for that fight (not the short slice), same combat truth locks.
- **No-pause** applies **during** each active combat level, not during these intentional between-level beats.

### Between-run loop
- retain meaningful growth
- reinforce identity direction
- re-enter with clearer intent rather than wider sprawl

### Long-term loop
- develop a creature fantasy over many runs
- deepen support identity and species meaning
- build toward later-scope ranch and world consequences only when the live RPG roguelite foundation is strong enough

The player should feel like:
- desperate prey at first
- a fast-learning survivor soon after
- a mutating predator in the middle game
- a terrifying force by late game
- a being whose choices help reshape the fate of the world by the final scope

# 3. Core Design Spine

## Pillar 1 — Combat timing trust
The timing system must be visually and mechanically honest.
The player must be able to trust:
- hit windows
- parry timing
- attack timing
- feedback
- lane pressure cues
- support triggers

Visible indicators and real timing windows must agree.

## Pillar 2 — Lane pressure
Lanes are a core part of the game’s identity.
They create:
- reading pressure
- prioritization
- threat tension
- movement commitment
- distinct timing challenges

The lane system should not be discarded lightly.
It is part of what makes this game itself.

## Pillar 3 — Bond vs Eat
This is one of the central identity splits of the entire game.

### Bond
- long-term value
- creature growth
- support identity
- future ranch access
- a more relational path to power
- investment in dangerous life

### Eat
- immediate predation
- selfish mutation
- direct body power
- short-term escalation
- ruthless self-prioritization
- using life as fuel

Both choices must remain valid.
The game should never make one of them obviously correct at all times.

## Pillar 4 — Run growth
Runs must evolve in a meaningful way.
Growth should come from:
- mastery
- combat pressure
- creature choices
- mutation choices
- support identity
- adaptation to what the run is becoming

The run-growth layer should feel like the run is changing shape, not just getting bigger numbers.

## Pillar 5 — Creature identity
Creatures must matter.
They should not be interchangeable reward tokens.

Creatures should eventually matter as:
- reward choices
- combat support partners
- ranch assets
- long-term training investments
- role-defining run pieces
- world/ecology identity carriers
- future progression anchors

## Pillar 6 — Creature support in combat
Bonded creatures should actively matter during combat.
They should:
- help the player
- reinforce good play
- feel like living support
- stay readable
- avoid replacing player skill

They must NOT:
- become autonomous screen-clearing pets
- flood the battlefield
- trivialize difficulty
- make the player irrelevant

## Pillar 7 — Mutation and predatory growth
The player’s growth must feel monstrous and personal.
Power should come from:
- what is eaten
- what is kept
- what is trained
- what is mastered
- what is chosen
- what kind of run is being built

## Pillar 8 — World oppression and boss spectacle
The world should feel oppressive and alive.
Bosses should feel like:
- events
- performance states
- world-twisting threats
- climactic identity checks
- territorial rulers of broken spaces

Bosses should eventually reshape:
- combat feel
- audiovisual tone
- rhythm/pressure patterns
- battlefield identity
- the larger world’s state

## Pillar 9 — Quig
Quig is a controlled mascot-guide.
He should:
- add humor
- add world flavor
- add useful commentary
- enhance readability and personality
- never flatten the game into parody

He works best in:
- tutorials
- reward moments
- boss warnings
- codex annotations
- strange commentary on player growth
- subtle world-state reactions

## Pillar 10 — Kaiju Ascension
The game must include a long-term kaiju growth line in which a dormant egg, embryonic catastrophe, sealed world-organism, or ancient bio-weapon gradually evolves into a giant world-ending final threat.

This growth should be shaped by:
- player choices
- creature choices
- world-state outcomes
- predator/bond behavior
- boss consequences
- ecological disruption or containment

The kaiju is not just a final boss.
It is one of the game’s primary meaning engines.

## Pillar 11 — Evolving World State
The world must change in response to:
- player predation
- player bonding
- creature population pressure
- boss outcomes
- corruption / stability pressure
- kaiju growth stage
- the player’s broader relationship to power

The world must feel like it is reacting, not just hosting the player.

# 4. Final Core Gameplay Loops

## Micro loop — second-to-second
- read lane pressure
- time attacks, parries, and dodges
- build combo/style
- survive enemy patterns
- use support and run-growth advantages
- stay in rhythm under pressure

## Combat loop — minute-to-minute
- defeat enemies
- gain mastery-driven EXP
- hit level-up moments
- choose upgrades
- strengthen player identity
- strengthen or express bonded support
- survive escalating waves
- face elites and bosses

## Run loop
- start weak
- build strength through mastery
- bond or eat creatures
- level and mutate the run
- unlock stronger support or predatory paths
- survive deeper phases
- confront escalating threats
- finish the run more defined than it began

## Long-term loop
- keep and train selected bonded creatures
- invest in support identity
- strengthen future run options
- deepen the bond/eat tension
- unlock a stronger ranch layer
- influence the larger world state
- shape the conditions that feed into the kaiju line
- push toward an endgame defined by the world you helped create

The combat system should support:
- lane-targeted attacks
- lane-targeted parries
- lane-aware dodges/movement
- timing-based success tiers
- combo/style growth
- ultimate payoff
- support triggers
- run-growth synergy

- readable

# 6. Final Run Growth Scope

## EXP system
The game should have a run-based growth system tied to mastery.

Good EXP sources:
- kills
- perfect timing
- successful parries
- combo/style milestones
- meaningful combat achievements

Bad EXP sources:
- passive waiting
- generic survival time
- background automation
- shapeless stat grind

## Level-up system
A run should include:
- periodic level-up pauses
- small choice drafts
- upgrades that shape the run
- growth that feels like mutation or adaptation

## Upgrade family direction
The first long-term categories should be:

### Flesh
- aggression
- consume power
- punish strength
- raw predation

### Bond
- support power
- support charge efficiency
- support synergy
- creature reliance

### Cadence
- timing reward
- combo/style acceleration
- flow-state play
- mastery amplification

### Survival / Instinct
- stamina efficiency
- damage smoothing
- recovery
- run stability

The final game may add more branches later, but these are the core early families.

# 8. Final Bonded Support Scope

## Active support rule
Only **one active bonded creature** should be in combat at a time, at least for the main early/mid scope.

Others may:
- remain stored
- provide passive value later
- be future bench/ranch assets
- but should not clutter the battlefield

## Support design rule
Support should be:
- triggered
- readable
- earned
- useful
- limited
- role-specific

Not:
- constant
- spammy
- autonomous
- main DPS

## First creature support roles

### Ashclaw
- offensive support
- lane-specific punisher
- amplifies aggression and strong timing
- best as a sharp follow-up striker, not a constant attacker

### Bond Remnant
- defensive support
- flow stabilizer
- helps recover from pressure without erasing failure
- best as a brief ward / smoothing / support pulse, not a permanent shield

## Support scaling rule
Support should scale through:
- bond level
- run upgrades
- support charge efficiency
- role specialization

It should NOT scale through:
- spawning more autonomous actors
- constant spam
- massive free damage

# 9. Final Creature Ranch Scope

## Status
Planned for later, not immediate implementation.

## Role of the ranch
The ranch should be a between-run creature hub that:
- stores bonded creatures
- allows training and long-term development
- lets the player assign one active support creature for the next run
- reinforces the tension between bonding for long-term value and eating for immediate power
- feels dark, strange, and in-world
- does not become a giant management sim

## What the ranch should eventually do
1. Storage
   - keep bonded creatures between runs

2. Training
   - improve support efficiency
   - deepen creature role identity
   - allow slow long-term growth

3. Assignment
   - choose one active support creature for the next run

4. Tension
   - keeping creatures should have long-term value
   - eating creatures should still matter as immediate power

## What it should NOT become
- a cute pet menu
- a giant spreadsheet
- a disconnected side mode
- a system that removes the value of eating creatures

## Ranch relationship to later systems
The ranch may eventually become one of the main places where:
- long-term creature identity is preserved
- support roles deepen
- world-state counterplay or preparation happens
- kaiju-related consequences are understood or resisted

# 11. Final Kaiju Scope

## Kaiju role
The kaiju line is a long-form consequence system and the game’s largest-scale escalation engine.

It should begin as:
- an egg
- seed
- larval horror
- dormant world-organism
- sealed embryo
- ancient bio-weapon
or another embryonic catastrophe form that fits the world.

## Kaiju growth stages
The kaiju should eventually progress through something like:
1. Dormant / egg state
2. Disturbance / hatching pressure
3. Juvenile regional threat
4. Mature apex destabilizer
5. Awakening catastrophe
6. Kaiju-class endgame final boss

## What shapes its growth
Its development should be influenced by:
- player choices
- creature bond/eat behavior
- ecological pressure
- corruption/stability trends
- boss outcomes
- what regions are protected, damaged, or sacrificed
- the player’s overall philosophy of power

## Why it matters
The kaiju is not just “the final enemy.”
It is:
- the long-term consequence of the game’s systems
- a mirror of player behavior
- a world-ending escalation
- a final boss whose meaning is earned, not arbitrary
