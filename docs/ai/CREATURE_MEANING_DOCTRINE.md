# CREATURE MEANING DOCTRINE — WHAT WE FED

## 1. Creatures are not Commodities
- Every creature species must have a distinct **Identity**, **Predation Payoff**, and **Bonded Support**.
- Generic naming is banned. Use evocative, dark names (e.g., `ashclaw`, `veilskin`).

## 2. Creature Grades: Tiered Lifeforms
- Creatures appear in multiple grades (Brood / Mature / Alpha).
- Grades affect: Strength, DNA value, desirability, and support potential.

## 3. Support-Role Specialization
- Creature support roles should deepen across distinct lanes:
    - **Cadence Amplification**: Rewards flow and timing.
    - **Survival Stabilization**: Helps recovery under pressure.
    - **Predation Payoff**: Increases feeding effectiveness.
    - **Pressure Control / Debuff**: Disrupts enemy lane authority.

## 4. The Bond vs Eat Tension
- This is the game's soul. Protect the split:
    - **Predation (Eat)**: Immediate high-value mutation/DNA. direct body power.
    - **Bonding (Bond)**: Long-term relational power, investment in dangerous life.
- A creature should raise the question: “Do I become part of you, or do I remain beside you?”

## 5. DNA Integrity
- DNA is species-specific. Evolution is not a generic level-up.
- `RunGrowth.gd` and `GameState.gd` must reflect this specific predation economy.

## 6. Support Readability & Presence
- Bonded creatures must have a **Presence** on the HUD or in the Lane.
- Visual: Symbol/flash of Cooler palette (Blue/Teal).
- Mechanical: A clear, readable action (e.g., "Ashclaw parried Lane 1").

## 7. Anti-Stat-Sludge
- Reject minor buffs (+1%/+2%). Every upgrade should be a meaningful jump.

## 8. Verification
- Evaluate by asking: "Does this feel like a monster or a spreadsheet?"
