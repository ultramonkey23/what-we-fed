# WORLD-REACTION KAIJU DOCTRINE V1

## 1. Doctrine Summary
The Living Codex is an anomalous intrusion, an uninvited cataloguer of sacred lineages. The world of *What We Fed* is not a passive ruin awaiting salvation; it is a wondrous, sacred, and fiercely reactive ecology. As the Codex harvests or bonds with the biome, the world mounts a massive, escalating immunological response. This response is the Kaiju—a physical manifestation of the world’s attempt to excise, contain, or test the foreign presence. Replayability is canon: every re-instantiation of the Codex triggers a new, adaptive global reaction driven by the player's cumulative predation or empathy.

## 2. Definition of the Kaiju
The Kaiju is not a reflection of the player's literal "evil," nor is it a preordained, mustache-twirling antagonist. It is the world's apex white-blood-cell—a colossal, hyper-evolved corrective body birthed from the immediate pressure the Codex applies to the ecosystem. It is an ecological antibody made of aggregated regional matter and dominant local DNA, shaped entirely by the degree of the player's disruption.

## 3. Why Every Run Has a Kaiju
Every run is a fresh *Intrusion* by the Living Codex into a reactive timeline. The sheer metaphysical weight of the Codex's arrival violently agitates the world’s stasis. As soon as the Codex begins absorbing DNA or bonding with creatures, the world identifies an existential anomaly and begins gestating a counter-force to balance the scales. To play is to provoke; therefore, every run guarantees a localized Kaiju gestation as the world attempts to reject the infection.

## 4. Kaiju Reaction-Type Categories
*   **The Purgative (High Eat/Predatory):** A violent, hyper-lethal exterminator forged from pure ecological trauma. Designed to crush, dissolve, and eradicate the Codex. Visually sharp, jagged, hyper-predatory, and merciless.
*   **The Containment (High Bond/Symbiotic):** A massive, isolating warden meant to quarantine the anomaly. Less focused on killing, more focused on suppressing and sealing away the Codex before its unnatural influence spreads to the local fauna. Visually overgrown, dense, slow, and suffocating.
*   **The Chimeric (Chaotic/Mixed):** A desperate, unstable amalgamation of conflicting regional lineages reacting to an unpredictable Codex. Erratic, mutated, self-cannibalizing, and unpredictable in its assault.

## 5. How Regions Bias Kaiju Form and Temperament
The Kaiju gestates using the local biome’s available mass and dominant species. A run focused in a fungal decay zone will spawn a spore-choked, parasitic colossus. A run ascending a crystalline peak will birth a shattered, glass-winged raptor. The region dictates the Kaiju's physical architecture, elemental payload, and arena, while the player's behavior (Eat vs Bond) dictates its intent and combat tactics.

## 6. How Bond vs Eat Influence Kaiju Evolution
*   **Eat-Heavy (Harvesting):** Triggers a terrified, highly aggressive evolutionary spike. The world feels its lineages being erased. The Kaiju develops thick carapaces, overwhelming predatory reach, and raw destructive power to neutralize a high-threat apex predator.
*   **Bond-Heavy (Assimilation):** Triggers a complex, confusing reaction. The world recognizes the Codex is assimilating its children into an artificial collective. The Kaiju becomes methodical—employing suppression tactics, mind-altering frequencies, and complex, labyrinthine attacks to sever the Codex's artificial bonds and reclaim its fauna.

## 7. How Potential Influences Kaiju Escalation
*Potential* is the expanding metaphysical footprint of both the player and the world. As the Codex's *Player Potential* deepens across dozens of re-instantiations, the world's baseline *Creature Potential* rises to meet it. The Kaiju no longer spawns as a reactive infant; it begins runs partially formed, the world remembering past eradication failures. This escalation ensures the 100th run faces an unfathomably complex and pre-adapted antibody compared to the 1st run, supporting endless, narrative-justified replayability.

## 8. What Should Be Surfaced Early
*   The world is alive, watchful, and not yet ruined.
*   The player is an admitted outsider—an artificial, uninvited anomaly.
*   The environment begins shuddering, shifting, or bleeding in direct response to the player's first harvests.
*   The ominous truth that an end-run threat is currently gestating somewhere specifically *because* of the player's actions.

## 9. What Should Remain Hidden
*   The precise, mathematical mechanics of how Potential scales the Kaiju's stats.
*   The ultimate tragedy that the Kaiju is simply trying to save its untouched world from you.
*   The full spectrum of extreme Kaiju visual permutations (save these for high-Potential, late-game runs).

## 10. What Should Be Promoted
*   The "Lair as an Interface Wound"—the only anchor point where the world cannot reach the Codex.
*   The stark contrast between "Harvesting of Lineage" (Eat) and "Forcing Symbiosis" (Bond).
*   The Kaiju as an ecological antibody, deeply tied to the specific biomes the player traverses.
*   "Start Weak, Become Feared" applies symmetrically to both the player *and* the world's escalating reaction.

## 11. What Should Be Softened
*   The concept of a "final boss." It is an *Eradication Attempt* by an ecosystem, not a villain with an evil monologue.
*   The "heroic" framing of the player's actions. Even on a pure Bond route, you are an invasive species forcing a foreign will upon the local fauna.

## 12. What Should Be Retired
*   The "Inner Demon" or literal "Dark Half" Kaiju concept.
*   "Chosen One" prophecies or pre-ordained "World Ending" stakes.
*   Generic apocalypse or pre-ruined world aesthetics (the player is the apocalypse).

## 13. What Should Be Deferred
*   Multi-Kaiju encounters (keep the focus on a single, massive, singular antibody response per run).
*   Deep-lore cosmology dumps explaining exactly *who* or *what* built the Living Codex. The mystery of the origin is less important than the immediate ecological consequence of its presence.
